Te Toi Māori Cultural Arts Festival

Celebrating Traditional Māori Arts, Performance, and Storytelling

Kapa Haka Whakairo Raranga Storytelling

Kapa Haka Performances

Experience powerful traditional Māori performing arts including waiata, haka, and poi. Festival groups from across Aotearoa gather to share their stories, history, and identity through movement and song.

Whakairo - Traditional Carving

Master carvers demonstrate the art of whakairo, shaping wood into meaningful cultural symbols that represent genealogy, history, and tribal identity. Visitors can observe carving techniques and learn about the cultural significance of traditional patterns.

Raranga - Flax Weaving Workshops

Learn about the traditional practice of weaving harakeke (New Zealand flax) into baskets, mats, and decorative items. Skilled weavers share knowledge passed down through generations.

Māori Storytelling Evenings

Gather around for an evening of storytelling where local knowledge holders share traditional pūrākau (stories), legends, and histories connected to the land and people of Aotearoa.
